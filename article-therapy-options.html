Understanding Therapy Options for Students

Therapy can be a valuable resource for students navigating mental health challenges. This guide explains different types of therapy, how to access them, and what to expect.

Types of Therapy

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)

Focuses on identifying and changing negative thought patterns

Best for: Anxiety, depression, stress

Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT)

Teaches emotional regulation and distress tolerance

Best for: Emotional dysregulation, self-harm

Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT)

Focuses on accepting difficult emotions while committing to values-based actions

Best for: Anxiety, life transitions

Accessing Therapy as a Student

  1. University Counseling Center: Free/low-cost sessions
  2. Student Health Insurance: Check coverage for therapists
  3. Community Clinics: Sliding scale options
  4. Online Platforms: Teletherapy options

What to Expect in First Sessions

Session Typical Focus Questions to Ask
1 Assessment and goals What's your approach? How often will we meet?
2-3 Building rapport What can I do between sessions?
4+ Active work How will we measure progress?

Key Takeaways

  • Different therapies suit different needs - shop around
  • It's normal to try multiple therapists before finding a good fit
  • Consistency is key - give it several sessions
