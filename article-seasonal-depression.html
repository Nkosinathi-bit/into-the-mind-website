Recognizing and Coping with Seasonal Depression

Seasonal Affective Disorder (SAD) affects many students, particularly during winter months. Learn to recognize symptoms and implement effective coping strategies.

Symptoms of SAD

  • Persistent low mood
  • Loss of interest in activities
  • Increased sleep and fatigue
  • Difficulty concentrating
  • Changes in appetite

Light Therapy

Using a light therapy box can help regulate circadian rhythms:

  1. Use within first hour of waking
  2. Position about 16-24 inches from face
  3. 10,000 lux intensity for 20-30 minutes
  4. Continue daily through difficult season

Vitamin D Supplementation

Many people with SAD have low Vitamin D levels:

Level IU Recommendation
Deficient (<0 ng/mL) 5,000 IU daily for 8 weeks
Insufficient (20-30 ng/mL) 2,000 IU daily
Maintenance 1,000-2,000 IU daily

Always consult a healthcare provider before starting supplements.

Key Takeaways

  • SAD is treatable - don't hesitate to seek help
  • Combine multiple approaches for best results
  • Plan ahead for challenging seasons
